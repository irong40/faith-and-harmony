# SOP: Construction Update Shoot

**Mission Type:** Construction Progress Documentation
**Typical Client:** General contractors, developers, project owners
**Frequency:** Weekly, biweekly, or monthly (per contract)
**Estimated Flight Time:** 20-35 minutes
**Deliverable Turnaround:** 24-48 hours

---

## Pre-Shoot Checklist

- [ ] Confirm shoot date/time with site superintendent
- [ ] Obtain site access credentials and parking instructions
- [ ] Review previous shoot photos for comparison angles
- [ ] Load saved flight plan (same waypoints every visit)
- [ ] Check weather — wind under 20 mph, no precipitation
- [ ] Charge batteries (minimum 2 full batteries)
- [ ] Pack PPE: hard hat, hi-vis vest, steel-toe boots, safety glasses
- [ ] Confirm any specific areas the client wants documented this visit

## Equipment

- **Aircraft:** DJI Mini 4 Pro (standard) or Matrice 4E (large sites)
- **Batteries:** 2 minimum, 3 recommended
- **Memory:** Formatted microSD with enough capacity (64 GB+)
- **Ground gear:** Hard hat, hi-vis vest, tablet/phone with DJI app
- **Optional:** Handheld gimbal for ground-level walkthrough

## Site Arrival Protocol

1. Check in with site superintendent or designated contact
2. Sign visitor/contractor log if required
3. Get briefing on active hazard zones (crane radius, heavy equipment, open excavations)
4. Identify safe launch/landing zone — flat, clear of debris, away from active work
5. Note weather conditions (wind, cloud cover, visibility)

## Shot List

### Aerial — Mandatory (same every visit)

| # | Shot | Altitude | Camera Angle | Notes |
|---|------|----------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | North oblique | 150-200 ft AGL | 45° down | Same position every shoot |
| 2 | East oblique | 150-200 ft AGL | 45° down | Same position every shoot |
| 3 | South oblique | 150-200 ft AGL | 45° down | Same position every shoot |
| 4 | West oblique | 150-200 ft AGL | 45° down | Same position every shoot |
| 5 | Top-down nadir | 200-300 ft AGL | 90° (straight down) | Full site coverage |
| 6 | Approach/context | 300-400 ft AGL | 30° down | Shows site in relation to surroundings |

### Aerial — Situational

| # | Shot | When to Use |
|---|------|------------|
| 7 | POI orbit (video) | Major structure — 360° orbit at 100-150 ft |
| 8 | Slow flyover (video) | Client requests video deliverable |
| 9 | Detail zoom-in | Specific area of concern (cracks, drainage, grading) |

### Ground-Level — Mandatory

| # | Shot | Notes |
|---|------|-------|
| 10 | Entrance/signage | Shows project identity and date context |
| 11 | Active work areas | Framing, concrete, MEP rough-in — whatever is current |
| 12 | Material staging | Stockpiles, deliveries, equipment on site |
| 13 | Problem areas | Anything flagged by GC or visible issues |

## Flight Execution

1. Launch from safe zone, ascend to 150 ft
2. Fly to **north waypoint** first, capture photo
3. Proceed clockwise: east → south → west
4. Ascend to 200-300 ft for nadir/top-down shots
5. Ascend to 300-400 ft for context/approach shot
6. If video requested: descend to 100-150 ft, run POI orbit around primary structure
7. Land, swap battery if needed
8. Ground-level walkthrough with handheld or drone at low altitude

## Deliverables

| Deliverable | Format | Description |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| Photo set | JPG (full res) | All aerial + ground shots, organized by angle |
| Comparison grid | JPG/PDF | Side-by-side current vs. previous shoot (4 cardinal views) |
| Progress video | MP4 (60-90 sec) | Flyover edit with date overlay and progress notes |
| Written summary | PDF or email | 3-5 bullet points on visible progress since last shoot |
| Raw files | DNG/JPG | On request — delivered via cloud link |

## Post-Shoot

- [ ] Back up all media to two locations before leaving site
- [ ] Log flight in Trestle (hours, batteries used, mission notes)
- [ ] Process comparison grid within 24 hours
- [ ] Edit progress video (if contracted)
- [ ] Write progress summary
- [ ] Deliver to client via agreed method (email, portal, cloud link)
- [ ] Archive shoot folder: `YYYY-MM-DD_[ClientName]_Construction_Update`

## Safety Notes

- Never fly over active workers
- Maintain 50 ft horizontal distance from cranes and heavy equipment
- If crane is operating, coordinate with operator for flight windows
- Watch for guy wires, temporary power lines, and scaffolding
- Wind can be unpredictable near tall structures — monitor throughout flight
