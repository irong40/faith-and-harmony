# SOP: Real Estate / Property Marketing Shoot

**Mission Type:** Real Estate Aerial Photography & Video
**Typical Client:** Realtors, property owners, developers, property managers
**Frequency:** One-time per listing
**Estimated Flight Time:** 15-30 minutes
**Deliverable Turnaround:** 24-48 hours (rush: same day +$)

---

## Pre-Shoot Checklist

- [ ] Confirm property address, listing agent contact, and lockbox/access info
- [ ] Get MLS listing details — beds/baths, lot size, key selling features
- [ ] Ask agent: any specific angles or features to highlight? (pool, acreage, waterfront, neighborhood)
- [ ] Check weather — golden hour preferred (sunrise or 1-2 hrs before sunset)
- [ ] Charge batteries (2 minimum, 3 for large properties)
- [ ] Review property on Google Maps for layout, lot boundaries, nearby landmarks
- [ ] Confirm no airspace restrictions at location
- [ ] Coordinate timing — property should be staged, cars moved, lawn mowed

## Equipment

- **Aircraft:** DJI Mini 4 Pro (residential) or Matrice 4E (estates/commercial)
- **Batteries:** 2-3
- **Memory:** Formatted microSD, 64 GB+
- **Camera settings:** 4K video, highest photo resolution, HDR if available
- **ND filters:** Use ND16-ND64 for smooth cinematic video (1/50 shutter at 24fps)
- **Ground gear:** Handheld gimbal or tripod for ground-level video

## Site Arrival Protocol

1. Meet agent or confirm property is accessible
2. Walk the property — identify best angles, selling features, potential obstacles
3. Move trash cans, hoses, or obvious eyesores if possible (ask agent first)
4. Confirm cars are out of driveway
5. Plan flight path to capture property in best light (sun behind camera)
6. Choose launch zone that won't appear in hero shots

## Flight Execution — Video Moves

All video at **24fps or 30fps**, use ND filters for motion blur.

1. **Reveal shot:** Start behind a tree/feature, slowly rise to reveal property
2. **Push-in:** 200 ft out, slowly fly toward property while descending
3. **Driveaway:** Start close and low, ascend and pull back to show full property
4. **Orbit:** POI locked on property center, 100-150 ft altitude, slow 360°
5. **Flyover:** Approach from street side, fly over property to backyard, show depth of lot

**Speed:** Slow and smooth. Max 5 mph for cinematic feel. No jerky movements.

## Photo Editing Standards

- Straight horizons (level every shot)
- Sky replacement only if client requests and approves
- Color correction for consistent warmth
- Remove lens distortion
- Do NOT digitally remove power lines, neighbors, or permanent features (ethical/legal)
- Green grass enhancement: subtle only

## Scheduling Tips

- **Best light:** Golden hour (1-2 hrs before sunset) or early morning
- **Avoid:** Harsh midday sun (deep shadows under eaves), overcast with no contrast
- **Coordinate with:** Interior photographer if doing combined shoot
- **Seasonal:** Spring/summer preferred for green landscaping
- **Wind:** Under 15 mph for smooth video

## Safety Notes

- Residential neighborhoods: be courteous, minimize hover time over adjacent properties
- Watch for power lines along streets and between houses
- Never fly over people (open houses, neighbors in yards)
- Check for dog runs and overhead wires in backyards before low-altitude shots
