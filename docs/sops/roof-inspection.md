# SOP: Roof Inspection Shoot

**Mission Type:** Roof Condition Assessment
**Typical Client:** Homeowners, property managers, roofers, insurance adjusters
**Frequency:** One-time or annual
**Estimated Flight Time:** 10-20 minutes
**Deliverable Turnaround:** 24-48 hours

---

## Pre-Shoot Checklist

- [ ] Confirm property address and client contact
- [ ] Ask client what they're looking for (storm damage, age assessment, leak source, pre-purchase)
- [ ] Check weather — clear skies preferred for detail shots, wind under 15 mph
- [ ] Charge batteries (2 minimum)
- [ ] Review property on Google Maps/Earth for roof layout, obstacles, power lines
- [ ] Confirm no airspace restrictions (TFR, controlled airspace) at location
- [ ] Notify client of approximate arrival time

## Equipment

- **Aircraft:** DJI Mini 4 Pro (residential) or Matrice 4E (commercial/large roofs)
- **Batteries:** 2 minimum
- **Memory:** Formatted microSD, 64 GB+
- **Camera settings:** Highest resolution, manual exposure if possible (avoid blown-out highlights on light-colored roofs)
- **Optional:** Thermal camera attachment (leak detection, moisture mapping)

## Site Arrival Protocol

1. Meet client or property contact
2. Walk the perimeter — note power lines, trees, antennas, HVAC units, skylights
3. Ask client to point out known problem areas (interior leaks, visible damage from ground)
4. Choose launch/landing zone with clear line of sight to entire roof
5. Brief client on flight plan and safety (stay back from launch zone)

| 3 | East face oblique | 40-60 ft AGL | 45° | |
| 4 | South face oblique | 40-60 ft AGL | 45° | |
| 5 | West face oblique | 40-60 ft AGL | 45° | |
| 6 | Ridge line detail | 30-50 ft AGL | 30-45° | Ridge caps, vents, flashing |
| 7 | Valleys and transitions | 30-50 ft AGL | 45° | Where roof planes meet |
| 8 | Penetrations close-up | 20-40 ft AGL | 45° | Vents, pipes, skylights, chimneys — each one |
| 9 | Gutters/eaves | 20-40 ft AGL | 60-70° | Gutter condition, fascia, soffit |

### Situational

| # | Shot | When to Use |
|---|------|------------|
| 11 | Damage close-ups | Visible damage — fly in to 15-25 ft, multiple angles |
| 12 | Thermal pass | If thermal camera equipped — systematic grid pattern |
| 13 | Moss/debris areas | Biological growth, debris accumulation |
| 14 | Flashing details | Around chimneys, walls, dormers |

### Ground-Level

| # | Shot | Notes |
|---|------|-------|
| 15 | Property front | Establishes the property |
| 16 | Visible damage from ground | Sagging, missing shingles, staining |
| 17 | Gutter discharge areas | Erosion, staining indicating drainage issues |
| 18 | Interior damage (if shown) | Client shows leak stains — photo for report correlation |

## Flight Execution

1. Launch, ascend to 100 ft for context shot
2. Descend to 60-80 ft for full nadir coverage
3. Fly each face at 40-60 ft, capturing oblique views
4. Drop to 30-50 ft for ridge, valley, and transition details
5. Fly each penetration individually at 20-40 ft (chimney, vents, skylights, pipes)
6. If damage spotted: hover at 15-25 ft, capture from 3+ angles
7. Fly gutter line at 20-40 ft
8. Return to launch, land

**Key technique:** Fly LOW and SLOW for roof inspections. Detail matters more than coverage speed.

## Condition Rating Scale (for reports)

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Excellent** | No visible issues, all components intact, 10+ years remaining |
| **Good** | Minor cosmetic wear, no functional concerns, 5-10 years remaining |
| **Fair** | Some wear visible, minor repairs recommended, 3-5 years remaining |
| **Poor** | Significant wear/damage, repairs needed soon, 1-3 years remaining |
| **Critical** | Active damage/leaks likely, replacement recommended immediately |

## Safety Notes

- Residential areas: be aware of neighbors, pets, children
- Watch for power lines running to the house — often at roof level
- Trees near roofline can obscure GPS and cause drift
- Never fly directly over people on the ground
- Steep roofs can create turbulence on the lee side in wind
